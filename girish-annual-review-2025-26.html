Chapter 1 — Risk Platform

Stepping Up When It Mattered

4-5 leaders resigned. The entire Risk platform landed on one person.

Before

  • Owned 1 environment (OCRR2) for Risk 2.0 migration
  • 5-6 team members under me
  • Other environments handled by separate leads
  • Multiple leaders across the risk org

After

  • Full ownership of all environments — UCLR2, Lending, TPAP, DeepApp
  • 18 team members — 3x growth overnight
  • End-to-end accountability for platform stability
  • Single point of leadership for the entire Risk platform
The challenge: Migration wasn't done by us. Steep learning curve. Frequent incidents. The system was in bad shape and needed someone to own it end-to-end.

Trained the expanded team to own their domains end-to-end. No single point of failure — team can handle anything independently.

Result: Stable environments everywhere. Reduced incidents. Team capable of handling Risk platform end-to-end without dependency on any single individual.

Chapter 4 — AI-First Culture

Building an AI-First Engineering Culture

3 years of AI adoption — from early adopters to org-wide transformation

The 3-Year Journey

Year 1 — Early Exploration

Started using AI tools when most teams hadn't even heard of them. One of the earliest adopters at Paytm.

Year 2 — Deepening Adoption

Integrated AI into daily development workflow. Built expertise and confidence.

Year 3 (This Year) — Socialization

Made it the team's mission to bring everyone along. Traveled to Noida, hands-on sessions, made AI usage mandatory.

Reflections

Key Learnings

Lessons from a year of ownership, building, and transformation

Leadership Lessons

  • Step up, don't step back — when others left, taking full ownership was the right call
  • Build the team, not just the product — a strong team outlasts any single feature
  • Accountability drives speed — owning the hiring pipeline made us the fastest to build a team
  • Lead the change you want to see — AI adoption worked because we lived it first

Technical Lessons

  • Inherited systems need patience — you can't fix what you don't understand first
  • Multi-tenant is hard but worth it — the agentic platform scales because of day-1 multi-tenancy thinking
  • AI as a multiplier — from code review to architecture, AI made a 5-person job doable by one team
  • Stability before features — nobody cares about new features if production is on fire
